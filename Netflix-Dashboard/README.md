Project Overview
This data visualization project explores the expansive library of Netflix, analyzing its content strategy over time. It provides a macro-level view of the streaming giant's catalog, focusing on the balance between movies and TV shows, global content production, and target audience demographics based on maturity ratings.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) Tracked

Total Titles: 8,807

Total Genres: 42

Total Directors: 4,528

Total Ratings Categories: 14

Key Visualizations & Business Insights

Content Type Distribution: A clear donut chart illustrates the platform's heavy investment in Movies (69.62%) compared to TV Shows (30.38%).

Growth Trajectory: A dynamic area chart maps "Content Added Over Time," showcasing the explosive growth and strategic shift in content acquisition from 2016 to 2020.

Audience Targeting: A bar chart breaking down total content by TV rating (TV-MA, TV-14, R) reveals that Netflix heavily targets mature audiences.

Global Footprint & Genres: Visuals detailing the Top 10 Countries and Top 10 Genres (Dramas, Comedies, Documentaries) highlight regional content hubs (like the US and India) and viewer preferences.

Business Value
This analysis provides crucial insights for content acquisition teams, producers, and media analysts looking to understand streaming industry trends, content diversification, and historical platform growth.
